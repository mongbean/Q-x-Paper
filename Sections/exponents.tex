\begin{lemma}
    $a^na^m = a^{n+m} \quad \forall a \in R,\, n, m \in \bZ^+ \cup \{0\}$
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    Let $S= \left \{ n \in \bZ^+ \mid \exists m \in \bZ^+ \cup \{0\} \text{ s.t. } a^na^m \neq a^{n+m}\right \}$. For the sake of contradiction, S is not empty. By the well-ordering principle, since $S\subset \bZ^+\cup\{0\}$, there must be a least element $l\in S$. First, for all $m \in \bZ^+\cup\{0\}$, 
    \begin{align*}
        a^0 \cdot a^m &= a^m \\
    \end{align*}
    Thus, $0 \notin S$. Therefore, by NIBZO $l>0$ and $l \in \bZ^+$. Then, $l-1\in\bZ^+\cup\{0\}$ but $l-1 \notin S$ since $l$ is minimal.
    \begin{align*}
        a^{l-1} a^m &= a^{l+m-1}\\
        a \m a^{l-1} a^m &= a \m a^{l+m-1}\\
        a^la^m&=a^{l+m}
    \end{align*}
    Thus, $l \notin S \xrightarrow{}\xleftarrow{}$. Then, $S$ must be empty.
\end{proof}
